AstroPay Casino Deposits in India 2026 — Full Guide

📅 Updated 4 May 2026 ✓ HindCasino Editorial Team

AstroPay is an e-wallet service designed specifically for cross-border payments to merchants that domestic payment rails sometimes block. For Indian online casino players in 2026, AstroPay is the most reliable third option after UPI and net banking — particularly useful when your bank flags gambling-tagged transactions. This guide explains how AstroPay works, its two main payment methods, fees, and when to use it.

What Is AstroPay?

AstroPay is a London-headquartered payment service provider founded in 2009. It operates a wallet plus a prepaid-card system, both designed for international merchant transactions. AstroPay is regulated as an Electronic Money Institution by the UK's FCA and partners with major card networks (Visa, Mastercard).

For Indian casino payments, AstroPay sits between your bank account and the casino. Your bank sees a transaction to AstroPay (a regulated e-wallet), not directly to the casino. This reduces the chance of gambling-MCC filtering blocking the transaction.

Two AstroPay Methods

1. AstroPay Direct (recommended)

Linked to your bank account. You authorise a one-off payment to the casino through the AstroPay interface — funds move from your bank to the casino via AstroPay's rails. No need to pre-load funds. Most casinos in HindCasino's listings support this method.

2. AstroPay Card

Prepaid virtual card you load with INR. You then use the card details at the casino's "Visa" or "Mastercard" deposit option. Funds come from the prepaid balance. Adds a step (loading the card) but is sometimes the only working method when bank rails are blocked.

How to Deposit Using AstroPay

AstroPay Direct flow

  1. At the casino's deposit page, select "AstroPay" as payment method.
  2. Enter the deposit amount in INR.
  3. You're redirected to the AstroPay portal. Log in (or create an account if first time).
  4. Select your funding source — bank transfer, UPI, or net banking through AstroPay's interface.
  5. Authorise. AstroPay debits your bank and credits the casino in seconds.
  6. Funds appear in your casino wallet within 1–5 minutes.

AstroPay Card flow

  1. Sign in to AstroPay app or website.
  2. Load INR onto a virtual card. Choose card amount (typically ₹500–₹50,000).
  3. Receive 16-digit card number, expiry, CVV.
  4. At the casino, select "Visa/Mastercard" deposit, enter the AstroPay card details.
  5. Transaction completes; card is single-use (some support multiple uses up to the loaded amount).

When to Use AstroPay

  • UPI repeatedly fails. You've tried GPay, PhonePe, and Paytm — your bank is filtering. AstroPay bypasses by routing through a regulated e-wallet.
  • Net banking transfer is blocked. Less common, but happens for some banks with strict gambling policies.
  • Large transactions above UPI limits. AstroPay supports ₹2 lakh+ per transaction at most operators.
  • You travel internationally. AstroPay works across borders without local payment-method changes.
  • You want a bank-statement buffer. Your bank sees an AstroPay transaction, not a casino transaction. Useful for privacy reasons (though banks can still identify AstroPay's merchant code).

Withdrawals to AstroPay

Casino-to-AstroPay withdrawals route back through the same flow. The casino processes the withdrawal request (subject to KYC), sends funds to your AstroPay wallet, and you transfer from AstroPay to your bank account. Total time end-to-end: typically 2–12 hours.

AstroPay withdrawal fees (casino → AstroPay) are usually free from the casino side. AstroPay charges 0.5–1.5% to transfer from AstroPay wallet to your bank.

AstroPay Account Setup

  1. Visit astropay.com and click "Sign Up".
  2. Enter email, phone number, set password. Phone OTP verification.
  3. Complete KYC — passport or Aadhaar, address proof, selfie. Takes 1–24 hours.
  4. Link a funding source (Indian bank account, UPI, debit card).
  5. Once approved, you can deposit at casinos via AstroPay Direct or load AstroPay Cards.

AstroPay's own KYC is separate from the casino's KYC. You'll do both. This is one of the friction downsides vs UPI/net banking (which only need the casino's KYC).

FAQ

Is AstroPay safe for Indian casino deposits?

Yes. AstroPay is regulated as an Electronic Money Institution by the UK FCA and partners with major card networks. Transactions are encrypted and PSP-grade-secure. AstroPay itself is not a casino — it's a payment processor between you and the operator.

Why use AstroPay instead of UPI?

Three main reasons: (1) when your bank repeatedly blocks UPI gambling transactions, AstroPay's wallet rails bypass that; (2) for larger transactions above UPI's ₹1 lakh per-transaction cap; (3) for some level of separation between casino activity and your bank-statement merchant codes.

Does AstroPay charge fees?

Yes. Typical fees: 1–2% on Direct deposits, 1.5% on Card loading. UPI and net banking are cheaper. The premium for AstroPay is reliability when other methods are blocked.

How long do AstroPay withdrawals take?

Casino-side processing: 1–12 hours. AstroPay-to-bank transfer: 1–6 hours additional. Total end-to-end: typically same day, sometimes next day. Faster than card withdrawals (3–5 business days) but slower than UPI's 1–24h window.

Do I need separate KYC for AstroPay and the casino?

Yes. AstroPay requires its own KYC (passport/Aadhaar, address proof, selfie) for regulatory compliance. The casino requires its own KYC separately. Both processes typically complete within 24 hours.

Is AstroPay better than crypto for casino payments?

For most players: AstroPay is simpler. You don't need crypto holdings, exchange accounts, or to handle wallet addresses. For high-volume players or those who already hold crypto: USDT TRC-20 has faster withdrawals (~30 min) and lower fees. See our crypto guide.
