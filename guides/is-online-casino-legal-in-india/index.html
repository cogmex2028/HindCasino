The Short Answer

India has no central law that explicitly criminalises an individual player for using an offshore licensed online casino. The Public Gambling Act 1867 — the only central legislation — targets operators of gambling houses, not players. Gambling is a State subject under the Constitution, and most states have not passed online-specific player penalties.

Three states have passed bans on online real-money games: Tamil Nadu, Andhra Pradesh, and Telangana. Two states have licensed frameworks for online skill games: Sikkim and Nagaland. Everywhere else, online casino play operates in a legal grey area — not explicitly criminalised, not explicitly permitted, and typically not enforced against individual players.

Tax is separate. Regardless of where you play, 30% TDS applies on net winnings from online games under Section 115BBJ of the Income Tax Act. This is a financial obligation on the player, not a legality of the game itself.

The Public Gambling Act 1867

India's central gambling legislation is the Public Gambling Act 1867 — a colonial-era statute that predates not just the internet but the light bulb. The Act criminalises:

  • Running or managing a common gambling house (Section 3)
  • Being found in a common gambling house (Section 4)
  • Owning property used as a common gambling house (Section 7)

The Act does not address online gambling, individual online play, or offshore operators — none of which existed in 1867. Importantly, gambling is listed as Entry 34, List II of the Seventh Schedule of the Constitution — meaning it is a "State subject" and each state can write its own laws. Most states have adopted versions of the 1867 Act with minor modifications.

Several states have updated their laws over the past decade to cover online gambling specifically. Some permit it (Sikkim, Nagaland), others ban it (Tamil Nadu, AP, Telangana), and most leave the question open.

States That Have Banned Online Real-Money Casino

Tamil Nadu

The Tamil Nadu Prohibition of Online Gambling and Regulation of Online Games Act 2022 bans online real-money games including casino-style games. The original 2022 Act was struck down by the Madras High Court but the Tamil Nadu government re-enacted it with revisions. Penalties for hosting include up to three years' imprisonment and ₹10 lakh fine; player-side penalties are present but less commonly enforced. Rummy and games of skill are partially exempt.

Andhra Pradesh

The Andhra Pradesh Gaming (Amendment) Act 2020 banned all online real-money games. The Act amended the AP Gaming Act 1974 to specifically cover "gaming through digital or electronic means involving the transmission of any data online."

Telangana

The Telangana State Gaming (Amendment) Act 2017 was the first such ban in India, criminalising online gambling games involving wagering. Telangana's framework treats all online betting for stakes — including skill games — as illegal.

If you live in Tamil Nadu, Andhra Pradesh, or Telangana: Consult a qualified local advocate before using any online casino, including offshore licensed sites. Penalties are typically targeted at operators, but individual liability under these state Acts is a legal question worth checking with counsel.

States with Licensed Frameworks

Sikkim

Sikkim was the first Indian state to license online gambling, under the Sikkim Online Gaming (Regulation) Act 2008. Licences are issued for sports betting and online gaming, but the framework restricts operations to within Sikkim's borders — limiting commercial usefulness for nationwide operators. Live casinos at the Sikkim Lottery centres are licensed and operating.

Nagaland

The Nagaland Prohibition of Gambling and Promotion and Regulation of Online Games of Skill Act 2016 created a licensing framework specifically for online games of skill — including poker, rummy, and fantasy sports — but excluding chance games (which remain prohibited). Nagaland licences are recognised in many other Indian states for skill-game operators, though their cross-border status remains legally contested.

Meghalaya

The Meghalaya Regulation of Gaming Act 2021 permits both games of chance and games of skill under a state licensing framework. The Act is one of the broader gaming regulations in India and has attracted both online casino and skill-game operators to register.

Goa, Daman & Diu

Goa and Daman have licensed land-based casinos under the Goa, Daman and Diu Public Gambling Act 1976. Online casino activity from Goa is not licensed under this Act; the licensing covers physical casinos only.

Skill vs Chance — Why It Matters

Indian gambling law distinguishes "games of skill" from "games of chance". Games of skill have historically received favourable treatment by Indian courts. The Supreme Court has classified Rummy (1968, State of Andhra Pradesh v. K. Satyanarayana) and Horse Racing (1996, Dr. K.R. Lakshmanan v. State of Tamil Nadu) as games of skill — meaning even in states with general gambling restrictions, these can sometimes be played legally for stakes.

Casino games (slots, roulette, baccarat) and most poker variants are generally treated as games of chance under Indian jurisprudence. Teen Patti and Andar Bahar have not been definitively classified but are usually treated as chance games in legal analysis. Live dealer casino games at offshore casinos operate under a casino licence in the jurisdiction of licence (Curaçao, Malta) — not under an Indian skill-games framework.

The mechanics — Section 115BBJ + Section 194BA

Two Income Tax provisions work together:

GST on Gambling

From 1 October 2023, the GST Council imposed 28% GST on the full face value of bets at online gaming, casinos, and horse racing. This is a tax on the operator that may be passed on to the player through higher rake, lower payouts, or fees.

Offshore licensed casinos are not GST-registered in India, so the 28% GST does not appear as a line item on your deposit. However, it does affect the operator's overall costs — which can influence published odds and bonus generosity. The GST regime has been the subject of significant industry pushback and litigation; the position may evolve.

How Enforcement Actually Works in 2026

Understanding actual enforcement helps put the legal grey zone in context. As of May 2026:

  • Domain blocking: Tamil Nadu, AP, and Telangana have issued ISP-level blocks for known online casino domains. Players in those states often use VPNs to access blocked sites — which has its own legal grey area.
  • Payment gateway pressure: the RBI and finance ministry have periodically issued circulars to banks asking them to scrutinise UPI transactions tagged for gambling. This is the source of occasional UPI deposit/withdrawal blocks, which players work around by switching apps.
  • Advertising: the Ministry of Information & Broadcasting has tightened rules on gambling advertisements. Casinos cannot directly advertise on Indian TV/print. Surrogate "news" or "social media" promotions remain common.
  • Individual player prosecution: we are aware of no reported case in 2025—œ2026 of an individual Indian player being prosecuted for using an offshore licensed online casino. Operator-side enforcement is far more common.

FAQ

Is online casino legal in India in 2026?

India has no central law explicitly prohibiting Indian players from using offshore licensed online casinos. The Public Gambling Act 1867 regulates physical gambling houses, and online gambling is a State subject under the Constitution. Tamil Nadu, Andhra Pradesh, and Telangana have banned online real-money games. Sikkim, Nagaland, and Meghalaya have licensed frameworks. Everywhere else operates in a legal grey area without specific player-side prosecution. This is not legal advice.

Do I have to pay tax on online casino winnings?

Yes. Section 115BBJ of the Income Tax Act taxes net winnings at a flat 30%, plus 4% cess. Since 1 April 2023, casinos must deduct 30% TDS on net winnings at withdrawal or financial year-end. You'll receive a Form 16A which must be included on your ITR. No slab benefit, no deductions allowed.

Which Indian states have banned online casinos?

Tamil Nadu (Tamil Nadu Prohibition of Online Gambling Act 2022), Andhra Pradesh (2020 amendment), and Telangana (2017 amendment) have all banned online real-money games. Karnataka's 2021 amendment was struck down by the Karnataka High Court in 2022.

What is the legal age to play online casino in India?

18 years is the minimum age at all offshore licensed casinos serving Indian players. Some physical Indian casinos (Goa, Sikkim) set the local age at 21, but online operators standardise on 18+. Age verification is part of KYC.

Can the government block UPI deposits to online casinos?

Banks can and sometimes do block individual UPI transactions tagged as gambling, particularly in restricted states. The RBI has issued circulars asking banks to scrutinise such transactions. There is no system-wide block of all gambling UPI traffic in India as of May 2026; blocks are intermittent and bank-by-bank. Players commonly work around occasional blocks by switching UPI apps (GPay Ã¢â€ ’ PhonePe) or using net banking.

If I win ₹1 crore at an online casino, what tax do I pay?

30% TDS on net winnings would deduct ₹30 lakh from your withdrawal, plus 4% cess = ₹31.2 lakh withheld. The operator issues Form 16A. You include this on your ITR; no further tax is owed under Section 115BBJ. Note: very large withdrawals may also require additional source-of-funds documentation under the operator's AML obligations.

Is using a VPN to access blocked casinos illegal in India?

India does not have a general law making VPN use illegal. However, using a VPN to access services banned by your state's law (e.g. online casinos in Tamil Nadu) does not change the underlying state law. The position is uncertain and varies; consult a qualified Indian advocate if relevant to your situation.

Are crypto casino winnings taxed the same way?

Section 115BBJ applies to net winnings "in cash or kind" from online games, which would generally include crypto winnings. Crypto-to-INR conversion may trigger additional tax events under separate crypto tax provisions (30% flat under Section 115BBH). Tax treatment of crypto gambling winnings is complex — consult a chartered accountant familiar with crypto tax.
