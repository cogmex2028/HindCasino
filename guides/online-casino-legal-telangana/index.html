Telangana was the first Indian state to ban online gambling, passing its amendment in 2017 — five years before Tamil Nadu followed suit. The state has maintained this position consistently, with the ban surviving court challenges. This guide explains the legal framework, the history, the current enforcement reality, and what Telangana residents need to know.

The Short Answer

Telangana has banned online real-money games under the Telangana State Gaming (Amendment) Act 2017. The Act:

  • Prohibits "wagering or betting on the outcome of online games"
  • Applies to both chance games (casino) and skill games (with limited carve-outs)
  • Imposes operator penalties up to 5 years' imprisonment and significant fines
  • Was the first state-level online gambling ban in India
  • Has been challenged in court but the substantive provisions have survived

For Telangana residents specifically: the legal position is restrictive. Offshore licensed online casinos technically operate outside Telangana's jurisdiction, but the 2017 Act may apply to individuals located in Telangana using such services. Consult a qualified local advocate before acting.

The 2017 Amendment Act

The Telangana State Gaming (Amendment) Act 2017 was passed in June 2017, amending the Telangana State Gaming Act 1974 to specifically cover online gambling. Key provisions:

  • Section 3: prohibits any form of "wager or betting" on online games involving stakes.
  • Operator penalties: imprisonment up to 5 years and fines.
  • Player-side provisions: participation in prohibited online gambling is criminalised under the Act, though enforcement against individuals has been limited.
  • Skill games included: the Act covers both chance games and skill games involving wagering, distinguishing Telangana from states that only restrict chance games.
  • ISP-level blocking: empowers the state to order ISPs to block known online gambling domains.

Why Telangana Was First

Telangana's 2017 ban predated similar bans in Andhra Pradesh (2020) and Tamil Nadu (2022). The state government cited several reasons for the early move:

  • Concerns about gambling addiction in Hyderabad's growing IT-corridor population
  • Reports of fraud and money laundering through online gambling platforms
  • Pressure from civil society groups to restrict access
  • The general legal grey zone for online gambling under existing federal law

Telangana set the legislative template that Andhra Pradesh and Tamil Nadu later followed.

Court Challenges and Current Status

The 2017 Act has been challenged in the Telangana High Court on various grounds, primarily related to skill-game inclusion. Unlike Karnataka's 2021 amendment (which was struck down in 2022), the Telangana Act has largely survived court review. The substantive prohibition remains in force as of May 2026.

Ongoing litigation may refine specific provisions over time. The general direction — prohibition of online real-money games in Telangana — has been consistent for nearly a decade.

  • Physical bookie operations: particularly during IPL season, Hyderabad-based satta operations.
  • Cyber-fraud investigations: online gambling sometimes overlaps with broader cyber-crime investigations.

  • Individual player prosecution: rare. No widespread campaigns against individual users as of May 2026.

Skill Games in Telangana

Unlike most Indian states, Telangana's 2017 Act explicitly extends restrictions to skill games involving wagering. This has affected Rummy operators, fantasy sports platforms, and similar services. RummyCircle and Adda52 Rummy have variously restricted Telangana access since 2017.
