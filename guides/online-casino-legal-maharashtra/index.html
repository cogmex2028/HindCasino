The Short Answer

Maharashtra has no online-specific gambling ban. The state's gambling laws — primarily the Bombay Prevention of Gambling Act 1887 — predate the internet by more than a century and don't address online gambling, online casinos, or offshore operators.

Most Maharashtra players access offshore licensed online casinos in a legal grey zone: there is no explicit prohibition targeting individual players, but there is also no licensing framework that legitimises the activity. This is the same position as most Indian states.

Tax position is separate and applies regardless: 30% TDS on net winnings under Section 115BBJ of the Income Tax Act.

Bombay Prevention of Gambling Act 1887

Maharashtra's primary gambling statute is the Bombay Prevention of Gambling Act 1887 — a Bombay Presidency-era law inherited at independence and amended several times since. The Act:

  • Criminalises running or managing a "common gaming house" (Section 4)
  • Criminalises being found in a common gaming house (Section 5)
  • Imposes penalties up to ₹500 fine and 6 months' imprisonment (modest under modern conditions)
  • Exempts games of "mere skill" — which Indian courts have interpreted to include Rummy and Horse Racing

Critically, the Act says nothing about online gambling. The framers in 1887 could not have anticipated the internet. Whether the BPGA's "common gaming house" definition extends to online spaces accessed from Maharashtra has been a subject of legal debate; no Maharashtra court has definitively classified offshore online casinos as falling within the Act.

Offline Gambling in Maharashtra

Maharashtra has not licensed land-based casinos. Goa is the nearest state with licensed offline casinos (operating under the Goa, Daman and Diu Public Gambling Act 1976). Maharashtra players who want in-person casino play typically travel to Goa.

Horse racing is licensed within Maharashtra — the Royal Western India Turf Club (Mumbai) and Mahalaxmi Race Course operate under specific permissions. Pari-mutuel betting on horse racing is legal and regulated. This is the only "casino-adjacent" licensed gambling in the state.

Lottery: Maharashtra paused state-run lottery operations in 2013. Online lottery sales from other states' legal lotteries are in a grey area.

Online Gambling Position in Maharashtra

Maharashtra has not passed an online-specific gambling law equivalent to the Tamil Nadu 2022 Act, the Andhra Pradesh 2020 Amendment, or the Telangana 2017 Amendment. There is no Maharashtra statute that:

  • Specifically prohibits online real-money games
  • Targets individual players
  • Requires ISP blocking of offshore casino domains
  • Imposes per-transaction penalties on banks processing gambling payments

The Maharashtra government has periodically discussed online gambling regulation, but no specific legislation has been enacted as of May 2026.

Tax on Winnings — Same as Rest of India

Maharashtra residents pay the same 30% TDS on net online gaming winnings as residents of any other Indian state. Section 115BBJ of the Income Tax Act applies federally — state of residence doesn't affect the rate.

Enforcement Reality in Maharashtra

As of May 2026, enforcement against individual online casino players in Maharashtra has been effectively absent. The Maharashtra Police's gambling enforcement focus has historically been on:

  • Illegal physical gambling dens (matka, satta operations)
  • Unlicensed cricket betting bookies during major IPL fixtures
  • Cryptocurrency-related fraud (separate enforcement track)

Individual players using offshore licensed online casinos are not a stated enforcement priority for Maharashtra Police. No reported cases in 2024–2026 of individual Maharashtra online casino players being prosecuted under the BPGA or any other statute.

This is the present situation; it is not predictive of future enforcement. If Maharashtra passed online-specific legislation similar to Tamil Nadu's 2022 Act, the position would change immediately.
