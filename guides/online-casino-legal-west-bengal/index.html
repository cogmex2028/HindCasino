The Short Answer

West Bengal has no online-specific gambling ban. The state's gambling laws — primarily the West Bengal Gambling and Prize Competitions Act 1957 — were written before online gambling existed and don't address it. Most West Bengal players access offshore licensed online casinos in legal grey territory: no explicit prohibition targeting individuals, no licensing framework legitimising the activity.

This is the same position as Maharashtra, Delhi, Karnataka, and most other Indian states. Tax position is identical across the country: 30% TDS on net winnings under Section 115BBJ.

West Bengal Gambling and Prize Competitions Act 1957

The primary West Bengal gambling statute. Key provisions:

  • Criminalises owning, keeping, or managing a "common gaming house"
  • Penalises persons found in a common gaming house
  • Penalties are modest (up to ₹1,000 fine and 3 months' imprisonment under amended wording)
  • Exempts games of skill (consistent with Indian gambling jurisprudence)
  • Has been amended several times since 1957 but never to specifically address online gambling

The Act predates the internet by 39 years. Whether the "common gaming house" definition extends to offshore online casinos accessed from West Bengal has not been definitively ruled on by Calcutta High Court. Most legal analysis treats individual online play as outside the Act's scope.

Online Gambling Position in West Bengal

The West Bengal government has not passed online-specific gambling legislation equivalent to the Tamil Nadu 2022 Act. There is no West Bengal statute that specifically prohibits online real-money games or targets individual players. Practical consequences:

  • No active ban on online casino games for individual players.
  • Offshore licensed casinos serve West Bengal players normally.
  • Indian skill-game operators (RummyCircle, Adda52 Rummy, Junglee Rummy) operate legally in West Bengal.
  • Enforcement focuses on physical satta and bookie operations, not individual online players.

Kolkata Context

Kolkata, West Bengal's capital and India's seventh-largest city, has a long history with traditional gambling: Teen Patti in family settings, festival-time card games, the well-known "Mahatabuk" lottery culture. Online gambling has rapidly displaced these for younger players in the city.

Kolkata-specific notes for online casino players:

West Bengal residents pay the same 30% TDS on net online gaming winnings as residents of any other Indian state. Section 115BBJ of the Income Tax Act applies federally. No additional West Bengal state-level tax on gambling winnings.

Could West Bengal Ban Online Gambling?

Possible in principle. The West Bengal government could pass online-specific gambling legislation similar to the Tamil Nadu 2022 Act. The political appetite, observable from public statements as of May 2026, is low — no specific ban legislation is publicly proposed.

If a national online-gambling framework arrives via MeitY (under discussion), West Bengal would be governed by that — replacing the patchwork state-by-state approach with uniform rules. The likely framework would distinguish skill games (regulated) from chance games (potentially restricted).
