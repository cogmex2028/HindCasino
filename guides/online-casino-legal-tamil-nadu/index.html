The Short Answer

Tamil Nadu has banned online real-money games under the Tamil Nadu Prohibition of Online Gambling and Regulation of Online Games Act 2022 (as re-enacted following Madras High Court challenges). The Act:

  • Prohibits online games involving "wagering or betting on the outcome"
  • Includes both chance games (casino) and historically applied to skill games (with carve-outs)
  • Imposes penalties up to ₹10 lakh fine and 3 years' imprisonment on operators
  • Includes provisions targeting players, though enforcement focuses on operators

For Tamil Nadu residents specifically: the legal position is restrictive. Offshore licensed online casinos technically operate outside Tamil Nadu's jurisdiction, but Tamil Nadu's Act may apply to individuals located in Tamil Nadu using such services. The 2022 Act and Its Legal Journey

October 2022 — Original passage

The Tamil Nadu Legislative Assembly passed the original Tamil Nadu Prohibition of Online Gambling and Regulation of Online Games Act 2022. The Act banned all online games involving wagering for stakes, including both chance and skill formats.

Late 2022 — Madras HC challenge

Skill-game industry operators (Junglee Games, Games24x7) challenged the Act in Madras High Court, arguing the inclusion of skill games (Rummy, Poker) violated Supreme Court precedent that classifies these as constitutionally protected under Article 19(1)(g).

Initial ruling

The Madras HC initially struck down the Act in 2023 specifically on grounds related to skill-game inclusion. The Tamil Nadu government appealed and subsequently re-enacted the Act with adjustments to address the court's concerns.

Current status (May 2026)

The Act is in force in its post-amendment form. It bans online chance games (casino) and applies restrictively to skill games. The exact scope of skill-game exemptions remains subject to ongoing litigation. Tamil Nadu is the most restrictive Indian state for online gambling as of May 2026.

What the Act Says

Key provisions of the current Act:

  • Prohibition of online gambling: any online game involving wagering or betting on outcomes for monetary stakes is prohibited within Tamil Nadu.
  • Operator penalties: persons or platforms providing online gambling services face up to 3 years' imprisonment and ₹10 lakh fines.
  • Advertising ban: advertising online gambling services in Tamil Nadu is prohibited.
  • ISP blocking: the Act empowers the state to order ISPs to block access to specified online gambling websites.
  • Player provisions: while penalties primarily target operators, the Act includes provisions related to individuals participating in prohibited online gambling.

Enforcement Reality

As of May 2026, Tamil Nadu enforcement under the Act has focused primarily on:

  • Domain blocking: ISPs in Tamil Nadu have been ordered to block known online gambling domains. Compliance varies; many blocks are bypassable.
  • Advertising enforcement: advertising of online gambling has been actively prosecuted.
  • Operator-side action: companies operating from within Tamil Nadu providing gambling services have faced prosecution.
  • Individual player prosecution: rare but legally possible. No widespread campaigns against individual users as of May 2026.

The enforcement landscape can change quickly. State legislators have signalled interest in stronger enforcement. Tamil Nadu residents should monitor local legal news and consult a local advocate before any online gambling activity.

Skill Games (Rummy) in Tamil Nadu

The legal position for skill games specifically remains contested. The original 2022 Act's inclusion of skill games was the basis for the Madras HC initial striking down. The current re-enacted Act attempts to distinguish skill games more carefully, but ongoing litigation will likely refine the boundaries.

Major Indian Rummy operators (RummyCircle, Adda52 Rummy, Junglee Rummy) have variously restricted Tamil Nadu access, accepted with disclaimers, or maintained pending litigation. Tamil Nadu residents interested in Rummy should consult a local advocate about the current position.

Tax Position

If a Tamil Nadu resident does play online (in violation of state law) and wins, the federal tax position is unchanged: 30% TDS on net winnings under Section 115BBJ. The federal tax provision applies regardless of whether the gambling activity is legal in the player's state.

Note: declaring gambling winnings on an ITR while located in a state that prohibits the underlying activity is a separate compliance question worth discussing with both a chartered accountant and a legal advocate.

Alternatives for Tamil Nadu Residents

Within Tamil Nadu's legal framework, online gambling alternatives that may remain available include:

  • State-licensed lottery — the Tamil Nadu state lottery is regulated and legal where state laws permit.
  • Fantasy sports — Dream11 and similar fantasy sports platforms have variously been treated as games of skill, though Tamil Nadu's position remains evolving.
  • Horse racing betting at licensed venues — narrow and specific exception.
  • Travel to other jurisdictions — Tamil Nadu residents visiting Goa, Sikkim, or international destinations can use licensed casinos legally in those locations.
