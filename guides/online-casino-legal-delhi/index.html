The Short Answer

Delhi has no online-specific gambling ban. The Delhi Public Gambling Act 1955 — the primary state statute — predates the internet and doesn't address online gambling. Delhi players access offshore licensed online casinos in the same legal grey zone as most Indian states.

Three things distinguish Delhi from other states for online gambling purposes:

  • NCT status means Delhi's legislative authority is limited compared to full states.
  • Central government proximity means policy discussions about national online-gaming regulation often surface in Delhi first.
  • Concentrated player base from financial services and IT sectors makes Delhi a meaningful market for operators.

Tax position is identical to the rest of India: 30% TDS on net winnings under Section 115BBJ.

Delhi Public Gambling Act 1955

Delhi's primary gambling statute is the Delhi Public Gambling Act 1955 — a state-level adaptation of the Public Gambling Act 1867. Key provisions:

  • Criminalises running a "common gaming house"
  • Imposes penalties on persons found in a common gaming house
  • Penalties are modest (up to ₹500 fine and 6 months' imprisonment under original wording)
  • Exempts games of skill (consistent with Indian gambling jurisprudence)
  • Does not address online gambling, online casinos, or offshore operators

The Act predates television, let alone the internet. Its application to online gambling is judicial interpretation territory — no Delhi court has comprehensively ruled on whether the DPGA covers offshore online casinos accessed from Delhi.

Online Gambling Position in Delhi

The NCT government has not passed online-specific gambling legislation. Unlike Tamil Nadu (2022 ban), Andhra Pradesh (2020 ban), or Telangana (2017 ban), Delhi has not extended the DPGA to specifically cover online gambling — nor has it created an online-gambling licensing framework like Sikkim or Nagaland.

The result: Delhi residents who play at offshore licensed online casinos operate in legal grey territory:

  • No explicit prohibition targeting them as individual players
  • No explicit permission via a licensing framework
  • No state-level enforcement targeting individual online casino players
  • Subject to all-India federal tax provisions (30% TDS)

Central Government Discussions

Because Delhi is the national capital, central government discussions about online gambling policy often surface here first. Recent developments worth knowing:

  • MeitY (Ministry of Electronics and IT) has been considering a national online gaming framework that would distinguish skill games (regulated) from chance games (potentially restricted).
  • GST Council implemented 28% GST on online gaming face value (October 2023) — affecting operators more than individual players.
  • RBI has issued circulars to banks regarding gambling-tagged UPI transactions.
  • Finance Ministry implemented Section 115BBJ 30% TDS via 2023 Finance Act.

These central-level developments affect Delhi players the same as players in any other Indian state — Delhi has no additional NCT-level layers.

Future Outlook for Delhi

Possible scenarios over the next 1–3 years:

  • Status quo continues: most likely. Delhi continues without specific online-gambling legislation, operating under the 1955 Act.
  • National framework arrives: if MeitY's proposed national framework passes, Delhi would be governed by that — replacing the patchwork state-by-state approach. Most likely to distinguish skill from chance games.
  • Delhi-specific ban: less likely. The NCT government has not signalled interest in a Tamil-Nadu-style ban.
  • Delhi-specific licensing: very unlikely. No political appetite for state-licensed online gambling in Delhi.
