Is Online Casino Legal in India?

This is the most important question for any Indian player, and the honest answer is "it depends on where you live and which site you use". India does not have a single, modern federal law that addresses online gambling. Instead, you have a patchwork of state laws, a colonial-era central act, and a constantly evolving tax position. Here's what you actually need to know in 2026.

The Central Law: Public Gambling Act 1867

The Public Gambling Act of 1867 is the only central legislation that touches gambling, and it predates not just the internet but the light bulb. It criminalises running a public gambling house — not the act of gambling itself, and certainly not online gambling, which the writers of 1867 could not have imagined. Crucially, gambling is a "State subject" under the Indian Constitution (Entry 34, List II), meaning each state can write its own gambling laws.

State-by-State Position

Position on Online Real-Money Casino States
Licensed / Permitted Sikkim, Nagaland (skill games), Goa, Daman
Grey area (no explicit ban; players use offshore sites) Maharashtra, Karnataka, Delhi, West Bengal, Kerala, most of India
Restricted / Banned Tamil Nadu, Andhra Pradesh, Telangana

The states in the third row have passed amendments banning online real-money games entirely. If you live in Tamil Nadu, Andhra Pradesh, or Telangana, you should consult a local lawyer before signing up at any online casino — even those licensed offshore.

Offshore Casinos and the Grey Zone

The casinos in our top five are all licensed and headquartered outside India — typically in Curaçao or Malta. They accept Indian players because there is no Indian law preventing an Indian individual from gambling on a foreign-licensed site, and there is no enforcement mechanism that targets individual players. This is the same legal grey zone that exists for crypto trading and fantasy sports in some states. We are not lawyers; this is not legal advice. If you are unsure about the position in your state, consult a qualified Indian advocate.

Tax on Winnings: The 30% TDS Rule

âš ï¸ Important — TDS rule changed in 2023 Since 1 April 2023 (FY 2023-24 onwards), 30% TDS is deducted on net winnings from online games. The casino must deduct this at withdrawal time, or at the end of the financial year on your balance. Section 115BBJ of the Income Tax Act governs this.

In practice: if you deposit ₹10,000, play, and finish the year with ₹35,000 in your casino account, you have ₹25,000 of net winnings, and ₹7,500 will be withheld as TDS. The casino issues a Form 16A. You'll need to include this on your annual ITR. There is no slab benefit and no deduction allowed — gambling winnings are flat-taxed at 30% plus 4% cess.

Casino Bonuses for Indian Players in 2026

Casino bonuses sound generous until you read the fine print. A "200% up to ₹2 lakh" offer can be worth less than a clean "100% up to ₹20,000" once you factor in wagering, max-bet rules, and game restrictions. Here are the four bonus types you'll see in the Indian market, and how to evaluate each one.

Welcome Bonus (Match Deposit)

The standard offer. The casino matches your first deposit up to a cap. The numbers you care about are: the match percentage, the cap, the wagering requirement, and whether wagering applies to bonus only or bonus + deposit. Always pick bonus-only wagering. A 100% up to ₹20,000 bonus with 30x bonus-only wagering is better than a 200% up to ₹50,000 offer with 45x bonus + deposit wagering.

Free Spins

Free spins are typically attached to a deposit bonus. Check three things: which slot the spins are valid on (Sweet Bonanza, Big Bass Bonanza, Book of Dead are the usual suspects), the value per spin (anything under ₹10 is throwaway), and the wagering on spin winnings (often higher than the cash bonus wagering — be careful).

No-Deposit Bonus

A small amount of bonus cash or free spins given just for signing up, no deposit required. Real no-deposit bonuses are rare in the Indian market because of high acquisition costs. When you see one, expect tight wagering (60x+) and a low max-cashout cap (often ₹2,000). Treat it as a try-before-you-buy, not a route to real winnings.

Cashback & Reload Bonuses

These reward existing players. Cashback returns a percentage of your weekly losses; reload bonuses match a smaller percentage of your subsequent deposits. Cashback paid in real cash (with no wagering) is rare and valuable — Casino Days's VIP program offers this. Reload offers are usually 25–50% match with the same wagering pattern as the welcome bonus.
