What Is Plinko?

Plinko is an instant-game casino category where a single ball is released at the top of a peg-board. The ball drops, hitting pegs and bouncing left or right semi-randomly at each level. After traversing all pegs, the ball lands in one of several slots at the bottom of the board — each with a different multiplier value.

Two key properties of Plinko outcomes:

  • Distribution is normal (bell-shaped). Most balls land near the centre. Outer slots are reached rarely but pay much higher multipliers.
  • Centre slots usually pay LESS than 1x. Yes — most ball drops result in a partial loss of your bet (e.g. 0.5x payout means you lose half your stake).

This is what makes Plinko statistically distinct from slot games: most rounds are "losses" but not zeros. You usually get part of your bet back. The big wins happen when the ball reaches the outer slots.

How to Play Plinko — Step by Step

  1. Set your bet amount. Minimum typically ₹10. Maximum varies by operator — usually ₹50,000 to ₹1,00,000 per ball.
  2. Choose risk level. Low, Medium, or High. This determines the multiplier distribution at the bottom slots (see Risk Levels section below).
  3. Choose rows (peg levels). Most Plinko games offer 8 to 16 rows. More rows = more variance and higher max multipliers, but more dispersion.
  4. Drop the ball. Click the "Drop" button. The ball traverses the peg-board with smooth physics-based animation.
  5. Outcome lands. Ball settles in a slot. The slot's multiplier is applied to your bet (e.g. 5x multiplier on ₹100 bet = ₹500 payout).
  6. Auto-play available. Most Plinko games support auto-betting with set stop conditions.

Risk Levels — Low, Medium, High

Plinko's risk selector changes the multiplier distribution at the bottom slots:

Low Risk

Centre slots pay close to 1x (e.g. 0.5x to 1.6x). Outer slots pay up to 10x at 16 rows. Most outcomes break even or lose small. Big wins are modest. Best for low-variance play.

Medium Risk

Centre slots pay 0.3x to 1.0x. Outer slots pay up to 200x at 16 rows. Balanced between hit frequency and payout size. Most common risk level played.

High Risk

Centre slots pay 0.0x to 0.5x. Outer slots pay up to 1000x+ at 16 rows. Long stretches of small losses punctuated by occasional very large wins. Highest variance.

The risk level doesn't change the long-run RTP — it just redistributes how the outcomes are paid. All three risk levels have the same expected loss per bet. What changes is variance: Low Risk gives you many small losses with small wins; High Risk gives you many big losses with occasional huge wins.

Plinko Strategy

Plinko has no in-play strategy decisions once the ball drops. Strategy is bet sizing and risk-level selection. The mathematically optimal approach:

  1. Pick the highest-RTP version available. BGaming or Spribe Plinko at 99% beats Pragmatic Play at 96.5% by 2.5 percentage points — meaningful over many rounds.
  2. Match risk level to bankroll. Low Risk: ₹100 bankroll handles 50-100 rounds. High Risk: needs 500-1000 rounds of bankroll to weather losing stretches.
  3. 16 rows usually > 8 rows. More rows = more variance and higher max multipliers. Same long-run RTP. More entertaining for big-multiplier chasers.
  4. Auto-bet with stop conditions. Set a stop-loss and stop-profit before starting. Don't manually click 200 times without limits.
  5. Don't "fix" the centre. No strategy adjusts the ball's trajectory. Centre-heavy outcomes are baked into the maths.

Plinko FAQ

What is Plinko?

Plinko is a crypto-native casino game inspired by the Plinko board from The Price Is Right TV show. A ball drops through a peg-board, bouncing left or right at each peg, eventually landing in a multiplier slot at the bottom. Outer slots have the highest multipliers (up to 1000x); centre slots have lower or sub-1x multipliers.

What is the Plinko RTP?

Plinko RTP varies by provider — typically 97-99% on the main bet. Provably-fair Plinko from BGaming and Spribe sit at 99% RTP. Spribe's Plinko XY is 97% RTP. Higher than most slots and competitive with the best table games.

Can I influence where the ball lands?

No. The ball's path is determined by the RNG / provably-fair seed. No timing, button-clicking, or other player input changes outcomes. Auto-bet produces the same long-run results as manual drops.

Why do centre slots pay less than 1x?

The centre slots are where most balls land (normal distribution). To keep overall RTP at 99%, the casino reduces the centre multiplier below 1x — meaning most rounds result in a partial loss. The outer slots' high multipliers compensate over many rounds.

Should I always play High Risk?

Only if your bankroll can handle long losing stretches. High Risk has more 0x or near-0x outcomes punctuated by occasional huge multipliers. Low Risk gives steadier small outcomes. Same long-run RTP across risk levels — pick based on variance tolerance, not "value".
