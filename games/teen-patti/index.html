What Is Teen Patti?

Teen Patti is a three-card gambling card game that originated in the Indian subcontinent and is widely played across India during Diwali and family gatherings. It belongs to the same family as British "Three-Card Brag" and is sometimes called "Flush" or "Flash" in casual play. The objective is straightforward: hold the highest three-card poker hand at the table, using a 52-card deck without jokers.

Online, Teen Patti is offered in two main formats. The first is the live dealer version, where a real dealer streams an HD video feed from a studio and you bet against the house according to the dealer's hand. The second is the RNG (computerised) version, where the entire game is run by a Random Number Generator and rounds resolve in seconds. Live Teen Patti is by far the more popular format among Indian players because of the social element and the casino-table feel.

Teen Patti Rules — How to Play Online

The rules of online Teen Patti are a simplified, dealer-based version of the traditional game played at Indian homes. Here's the step-by-step flow at a typical live Teen Patti table:

  1. Choose a table. Bet limits vary widely. Most tables list minimum bets from ₹50, with VIP tables going up to ₹5,00,000 per hand. Pick a table that fits your bankroll.
  2. Place the ante bet. The ante (or "boot") is the mandatory bet that triggers the round. You can also place optional side bets (Pair Plus, 6 Card Bonus) before cards are dealt.
  3. Cards are dealt. The dealer deals three cards face-down to the player position and three to themselves. Unlike home Teen Patti, you don't play against other players in live online — you play against the dealer's hand.
  4. View your cards. You see your three cards. In some variants ("blind play") you can choose to bet without looking — this halves your bet but means you don't know what you have.
  5. Bet or fold. If you think your hand will beat the dealer, place the "Play" bet (typically 1Ã— to 2Ã— the ante). If you don't, fold and forfeit the ante.
  6. Compare hands. The dealer reveals their cards. The dealer typically needs at least a "Queen-high" to qualify. If the dealer doesn't qualify, the player wins even money on the ante and the Play bet pushes. If the dealer qualifies and you win, both ante and Play pay even money (with bonus multipliers for premium hands).
  7. Side bets resolve independently regardless of the main game outcome.
Blind vs Seen play Traditional Teen Patti has a "blind" mechanic where you bet without seeing your cards in exchange for half-stakes. Most online live Teen Patti versions simplify this — you always see your cards. SuperSpade's tables do offer a blind-bet side wager for players who want the home-game feel.

Teen Patti Side Bets Explained

Side bets are optional wagers placed alongside (or instead of) the main ante. They typically pay much higher odds — but at significantly worse RTP. Understand each side bet before placing it.

Pair Plus

Pays if your three-card hand contains a Pair or better. Typical paytable: Pair 1:1, Flush 4:1, Straight 6:1, Trio 30:1, Straight Flush 40:1. House edge ~7%.

6 Card Bonus

Pays based on the best five-card poker hand made from your 3 cards + the dealer's 3 cards. Pays 7:1 for a flush up to 1000:1 for a Royal Flush. House edge ~8%.

Lucky 7 (SuperSpade)

Pays based on the total value of your three cards. Specific totals (typically 17, 21, 24) pay outsized multipliers. House edge ~9%.

Match the Cards

Pays if any of your cards match the rank of any of the dealer's cards. Higher payouts for more matches.

Basic Teen Patti Strategy

Teen Patti has a much smaller decision tree than poker — you mostly choose whether to play or fold based on your three-card hand. Here are the simplest strategy rules:

  • Always play if you have a Pair or better. The dealer needs Queen-high to qualify; any pair beats most qualifying hands.
  • Always play with King-high or Ace-high. These hands beat the qualifying threshold.
  • Fold Jack-high or lower (unless the side-bet structure changes the math at your specific table).
  • Queen-high is the borderline. Some tables favour playing it; others marginal-fold. Q-J-6 or higher is generally a play; Q-7-2 is a borderline fold.
  • Ignore side bets if you're playing for value. Their long-term return is significantly worse than the main game.

Live Teen Patti is largely a mathematical game against the dealer — there's no opponent to bluff or read. Stick to the rules above and you'll hit roughly the published RTP of 96–97% over the long run.
