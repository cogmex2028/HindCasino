What Is Aviator?

Aviator is a "crash" or "social multiplier" game developed by Spribe and launched in 2019. The format is radically simple: each round, a virtual plane takes off and a multiplier (starting at 1.00x) starts climbing. At a random point, the plane flies away and the round ends. Your job is to cash out before it flies — locking in your bet × multiplier as winnings. Cash out late, and you lose your bet entirely.

Three things made Aviator the breakout casino game of 2023–2026 in India and globally:

  • Speed: rounds last 10–30 seconds. You can play 100+ rounds per hour.
  • Social element: you see other players' bets and cash-outs in real time. Big multipliers and big losses both happen visibly.
  • Provably-fair maths: the round outcome is generated by a cryptographic seed published before the round — you can verify mathematically that the casino didn't manipulate the result.

Aviator is published by Spribe, certified by independent agencies (iTech Labs), and listed at every major Indian-facing online casino in 2026. RTP is 97%.

How to Play Aviator — Step by Step

  1. Open the Aviator game lobby. Aviator is in the "Crash" or "Instant" category at most casinos. Some operators feature it on the home page.
  2. Choose your bet amount. Minimum is typically ₹10. Maximum is usually ₹50,000–₹1,00,000 per round. You can place two simultaneous bets per round (more on this below).
  3. Wait for the round to start. Each new round begins after a brief 5-second window. The previous round's final multiplier is shown alongside the in-game chat.
  4. Plane takes off, multiplier climbs. The multiplier starts at 1.00x and climbs continuously. Climbing speed accelerates as the multiplier goes higher.
  5. Cash out before the plane flies away. Tap "Cash Out" at any point to lock in your bet × current multiplier. Example: a ₹500 bet cashed out at 2.5x pays ₹1,250.
  6. If the plane flies away first, you lose. The round ends instantly with an animation of the plane disappearing. Your stake is lost.
  7. Round resolves, next round begins. The previous multiplier joins the history bar at the top of the screen. The cycle repeats.

Two things to notice. First: cashing out at 1.10x wins ~88% of rounds, but pays so little it doesn't substantially grow your bankroll. Expected value of a ₹100 bet at any target multiplier is ₹97 — same whether you cash out at 1.10x or 100x. What changes is the variance: low-multiplier strategies win small most rounds; high-multiplier strategies lose most rounds and win big rarely.

Provably Fair — What It Actually Means

Aviator's headline trust feature is its "provably-fair" mechanism. The round's outcome (the multiplier at which the plane flies away) is generated by three combined cryptographic inputs:

  • Server seed: generated by Spribe before the round, published as a hash (so it's committed but hidden).
  • Player seeds: generated by clients of three players in the round, contributed live.
  • Round ID: a public counter.

After the round ends, the server seed is revealed. Anyone can independently compute the same SHA-256 hash of (server seed + player seeds + round ID), apply Spribe's published formula, and verify that the round result matches. Because the server seed was committed (hashed) before the round began, the casino can't retroactively change it.

What provably-fair doesn't change Provably-fair is a randomness verification mechanism. It guarantees the round outcome is genuinely random given the inputs — not manipulated. It does not change the underlying 3% house edge. You still lose 3% of every wager on average over the long run.

Aviator Features — Auto-Bet, Auto-Cashout, Two Simultaneous Bets

Two simultaneous bets per round

The Aviator UI shows two bet panels side by side. You can place two independent bets on the same round with different stakes and different auto-cashout targets. A common pattern: one small bet with high cash-out target (chasing a multi-x win), one larger bet with low cash-out target (locking in steady small wins).

Auto-bet

Configure Aviator to bet a fixed amount automatically on every round, with optional stop conditions: stop after N rounds, stop on profit/loss threshold, stop on a specific multiplier crash. Useful for systematic play; dangerous if you stop watching.

Auto-cashout

Set a target multiplier (e.g. 2.00x). When the multiplier reaches that figure, your bet is cashed out automatically. Removes the emotional component of deciding when to bail. Many serious Aviator players use auto-cashout at 1.5x–2.5x as a baseline.

Round history

The top of the screen shows the past ~10 round outcomes. Aviator players often try to "spot patterns" in this history. There are no patterns — see the myths section below.

Aviator Strategy — What Actually Works

Important upfront: no Aviator strategy beats the 3% house edge in the long run. Any approach that claims to do so is either misunderstood, dishonestly marketed, or both. What strategy can do is shape your variance — how often you win, how big when you do, and how long your bankroll lasts.

Strategy 1: Low-multiplier autocash (low variance)

Set auto-cashout at 1.30x–1.50x. Win ~65–77% of rounds with small returns. Bankroll moves up and down slowly. Risk of busting in any single session is low. Long-run return remains 97% of wagered amount.

Strategy 2: Two-bet split

Place two bets per round. Bet 1: large stake, low auto-cashout (1.30x) — covers downside. Bet 2: small stake, high auto-cashout (5x–10x) — chases upside. Variance is smoother than chasing only the high multipliers.

Strategy 3: Stop-loss + stop-profit

Set a session stop-loss (e.g. lose ₹2,000 → stop) and a session stop-profit (e.g. win ₹2,000 → stop). Doesn't change long-run EV but keeps you from spiral-betting after a bad streak. The single most useful habit for any chance-game player.

Strategies that do NOT work

  • Martingale (double after every loss): works mathematically only if the casino has no max bet AND you have infinite bankroll. Real casinos have both limits. Martingale loses spectacularly when you hit the bet cap during a losing streak.
  • "Pattern reading": rounds are independent. Past outcomes do not predict future ones. The history bar is for entertainment.
  • "Signal" or "predictor" apps: none work. The server seed is committed before the round and cannot be predicted. Apps claiming to predict Aviator outcomes are scams.

Aviator Myths — Common Misconceptions

Myth: "If the last 5 rounds crashed below 2x, a big multiplier is coming." Reality: rounds are independent. The probability of the next round reaching any multiplier is the same regardless of past results. This is the classic Gambler's Fallacy.
Myth: "Aviator hacks / signals / predictor apps work." Reality: they do not. Server seeds are cryptographically committed before the round. Apps marketing "predictions" are uniformly scams — typically luring you into paying for a Telegram channel that posts random multipliers and claims the wins while ignoring the losses.
Myth: "Higher stakes hit higher multipliers." Reality: bet size has zero impact on outcome. The multiplier is determined by the seed, not by what you wagered.
Myth: "Cashing out earlier improves long-run profit." Reality: long-run RTP is 97% at every cash-out point. Cashing out at 1.10x vs 10x changes variance, not expected value. Pick the cash-out level that matches your tolerance for losing streaks.
