What Is Andar Bahar?

Andar Bahar (also called Katti, Maang Patta, or simply "inside-outside") is a traditional Indian card game played for generations across the subcontinent. The structure is one of the simplest in casino gambling: a single card is revealed (the "Joker"), and players bet on whether the next card of the same rank will appear on the Andar (inside) pile or the Bahar (outside) pile. The dealer then alternates cards between the two piles until a matching rank is dealt. Whichever pile gets the match first wins.

The simplicity is exactly why Andar Bahar dominates evening live casino sessions in tier-2 and tier-3 Indian cities. There is no strategy decision to make once your bet is placed. You either get the match on your side or you don't. Rounds typically take 30–60 seconds end-to-end.

Andar Bahar Rules — Step by Step

  1. The dealer shuffles a standard 52-card deck. Live online Andar Bahar uses an automatic shuffler shown on camera. RNG-only variants use a certified Random Number Generator.
  2. The Joker card is revealed. One card is placed face-up in the centre of the table. This is the "Joker" — the rank you're trying to match. Suit doesn't matter.
  3. Place your bet. Bet on Andar (inside) or Bahar (outside). You can also place optional side bets (see below) during the betting window — typically 10–20 seconds.
  4. The deal begins. The dealer places cards alternately on the Andar pile and the Bahar pile. Tradition: if the Joker is black, the first card after the Joker goes to Andar; if red, to Bahar. Some live versions reverse this; check the table card.
  5. First match wins. The dealer continues alternating until a card matching the Joker rank appears. Whichever pile receives the matching card first is the winning side.
  6. Bets are paid. Winning Andar/Bahar bets pay roughly even money (varies by variant — usually 0.9:1, 0.95:1, or 1:1 depending on whether the Joker side started). Side bets resolve independently.
Why the payouts aren't full even money Live Andar Bahar typically pays 0.9:1 on the "first card" side and 1:1 on the other. This asymmetry is the casino's edge — the side that gets the first card has a slightly higher probability of winning, so it pays slightly less.

Andar Bahar Side Bets Explained

Most live Andar Bahar tables offer optional side bets alongside the main Andar/Bahar wager. They generally carry higher house edges, but provide more action.

Match in 1–5 cards

Pays ~2.5:1. The match arrives within five dealt cards from the Joker. House edge ~7%.

Match in 6–10 cards

Pays ~3.5:1. Match arrives between 6th and 10th card. House edge ~7%.

Match in 11–15 cards

Pays ~5:1. Less common, slightly worse EV. House edge ~8%.

Joker is Ace

Pays 11:1 if the Joker revealed is an Ace. House edge ~7.7%.

Joker is Red / Black

Even money. The first card revealed will be either red or black. Hardly worth playing — house edge zero on this bet at most providers.

Royal Match

Pays if both matching cards (Joker + the match) are Kings, Queens, or Jacks. Pays ~25:1.

Like all casino side bets, these are entertainment-priced rather than value-priced. If your goal is to play with the best long-run return, stick to the main Andar/Bahar bet.

Strategy — There Isn't One (And Why That's Fine)

Andar Bahar has no strategic decisions once your bet is placed. The cards are dealt; you either win or lose. No "basic strategy" exists, no card-counting works (the deck is reshuffled every round in most variants), and chasing previous results is the gambler's fallacy.

What you can control:

  • Bet on the right side of the asymmetry. If the Joker is black and Andar receives the first card at this table, betting Andar gives you the slightly lower house edge despite the lower payout.
  • Skip side bets. The main bet has a 2.15% house edge. Side bets average 6–8%. Sticking to main bets gives you 3Ã— the long-run return.
  • Manage bet size. Rounds are fast (30–60 seconds), meaning hourly turnover is high. A ₹500 bet every minute is ₹30,000 per hour — set a session limit accordingly.

Is Online Andar Bahar Legal in India?

Andar Bahar FAQ

What does Andar Bahar mean?

Andar means "inside" and Bahar means "outside" in Hindi. The game is a simple inside/outside card-prediction game where you bet on which side a card matching the dealer's Joker will appear first.

What is the house edge on Andar Bahar?

The main Andar/Bahar bet typically has a house edge of around 2.15% (RTP ~97.85%) — competitive with European Roulette and better than American Roulette. Side bets carry higher house edges of 5–8%.

Live Andar Bahar from licensed providers (Ezugi, Pragmatic Play, SuperSpade) uses standard 52-card decks with no manipulation. Live dealer formats are audited by independent agencies like iTech Labs and GLI.

Can you count cards in Andar Bahar?

No. Most live Andar Bahar variants reshuffle the deck before each round, making card counting useless. RNG variants use a fresh virtual shuffle for each round.

What's the difference between Andar Bahar and Katti?

Katti is one of several regional names for Andar Bahar — particularly common in South India. The rules are essentially identical. Maang Patta is another regional name. Online casinos universally use the term "Andar Bahar".
