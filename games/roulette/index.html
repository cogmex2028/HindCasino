How Roulette Works

The roulette wheel has 37 numbered pockets (European) or 38 (American). Numbers 1–36 alternate red and black in a specific pattern; the zero (and double-zero in American) is green. A ball is spun in the opposite direction to the wheel and eventually settles in one pocket. That number is the winning number.

You bet by placing chips on the table layout. Bets are accepted until the croupier (or live dealer) calls "no more bets." When the ball lands, all losing bets are swept, winning bets are paid at the published odds.

Online live roulette streams from a studio — Evolution Gaming and Pragmatic Live are the two dominant providers serving Indian casinos. RNG (computerised) roulette uses a certified random number generator and skips the live-dealer element. Live is preferred by most players for trust reasons and the social element.

European vs American Roulette — Always Pick European

The single most important roulette decision European roulette has one zero. American roulette has two zeros. The extra zero nearly doubles the house edge. Always play European where available — same game, half the disadvantage.

French Roulette and La Partage

French Roulette adds the "La Partage" rule: when the ball lands on zero, even-money bets (red/black, odd/even, high/low) lose only half their stake instead of the full amount. This effectively halves the house edge on those bets from 2.70% to 1.35% — competitive with Blackjack with basic strategy. Available at Casino Days and BigBaazi.

RTP & Bet Selection Math

Important insight: in European Roulette, every bet has the same house edge of 2.70% (with a couple of small exceptions like the five-number bet in American). Whether you bet ₹100 on red or ₹100 on number 17 or ₹100 split across two corners, your expected loss over the long run is identical: ₹2.70.

What changes between bet types is variance. Outside bets win ~49% of rounds but pay 1:1. Straight-up bets win 2.70% of rounds but pay 35:1. Pick the variance profile that matches your bankroll and entertainment preference, not because you think one bet is "better odds" than another (they aren't).

Roulette "Systems" — Reality Check

Martingale (double after each loss)

Bet ₹100 on red. Lose, bet ₹200. Lose, bet ₹400. Lose, bet ₹800… eventually red comes up and you recover all losses plus ₹100 profit.

Why it fails: table maximums. Most roulette tables cap outside bets at 1000× the minimum (e.g. ₹50 min → ₹50,000 max). A 10-round losing streak (red ~0.1% probability) means your 11th bet would need to be ₹51,200 — likely above the cap. You then can't recover and you've lost ₹51,150 chasing a ₹100 win. Mathematically, Martingale guarantees neither profit nor break-even.

Fibonacci (Fibonacci sequence stake escalation)

Same family as Martingale but with slower escalation. Same fundamental flaw — table maximums kill the system on long losing streaks. House edge unchanged.

D'Alembert (incremental escalation)

Increase stake by one unit after a loss, decrease by one unit after a win. Less aggressive than Martingale. Smoother bankroll curve, but same fundamental flaw — long losing streaks compound losses faster than recoveries.

Labouchère (cancellation system)

Write out a sequence of numbers summing to your target profit. Bet the first + last numbers. If you win, cross them off; if you lose, add the bet to the end. Same theoretical problem — losses compound fast on bad runs.

The honest truth about roulette systems No betting system overcomes a negative expected value. Math says so. Anyone selling you a "guaranteed roulette strategy" is either misunderstood, dishonest, or both. The only way to improve your roulette EV is to play European (not American), and ideally French with La Partage on even-money bets.

Roulette FAQ

What is the difference between European and American roulette?

European roulette has one zero (37 pockets total) and a house edge of 2.70%. American roulette has two zeros (38 pockets) and a house edge of 5.26% — nearly double. Always play European where available.

What is Lightning Roulette?

Lightning Roulette is Evolution's live roulette variant with random number multipliers (50x to 500x) applied to 1–5 numbers each round. RTP is 97.30% on straight-up bets, 96.13% on other bets. Significantly more popular than standard European Roulette at Indian-facing operators in 2026.

Does the Martingale strategy work in roulette?

No. Martingale (double after every loss) fails in real casinos because of table maximums. A losing streak hits the bet cap before you've recovered your losses. Mathematical analysis shows Martingale guarantees neither profit nor break-even.

Are all roulette bets equal in expected value?

In European Roulette, yes — every standard bet has the same 2.70% house edge. Whether you bet red, a corner, or a straight-up number, your long-run expected loss per ₹100 wagered is ₹2.70. What changes is variance (how often you win vs how much you win).

Is online roulette rigged?

Live roulette from licensed providers (Evolution, Ezugi, Pragmatic Live) uses physical wheels in monitored studios. RNG roulette uses certified Random Number Generators audited by independent agencies (eCOGRA, GLI, iTech Labs). No, it's not rigged at any legitimate operator.

What is La Partage?

A rule in French Roulette: when the ball lands on zero, even-money bets (red/black, odd/even, high/low) lose only half their stake instead of the full amount. This effectively halves the house edge on those bets to 1.35%. Look for French Roulette tables at Casino Days and BigBaazi.

Can dealers manipulate the wheel?

No. Live casino studios use weighted, balanced wheels monitored by independent auditors. Dealers do not have meaningful control over where the ball lands — physics, ball-spin speed, and wheel speed all interact too unpredictably.
