What Is Sic Bo?

Sic Bo is one of the oldest dice games in the world — originating in ancient China and played in Macau, Las Vegas, and now online casinos worldwide. The mechanics are simple: three dice are rolled in a sealed shaker; you bet on the outcomes before the roll; winning bets are paid based on the published paytable.

Three things make Sic Bo appealing to live casino players in 2026:

  • Variety of bets. Over 50 different bet options per round — from simple Big/Small to specific triples paying 180:1.
  • Fast pace. 25–30 second rounds, similar to Dragon Tiger.
  • Visible randomness. The dice roll is visually transparent — players can see exactly what was rolled.

How to Play Sic Bo — Step by Step

  1. The dealer prepares the shaker containing three standard six-sided dice.
  2. Betting window opens for 10–20 seconds. You place bets on the displayed betting table — covering any combination of bet types.
  3. Dealer shakes the dice in the sealed shaker for several seconds, then reveals the result.
  4. All three dice values are shown on screen alongside the betting table.
  5. Bets resolve simultaneously. All your bets are checked against the result; winners paid, losers cleared. There's no "decision" phase.
  6. New round begins. Typical cycle: 25–30 seconds end-to-end.

Sic Bo Strategy — Bet Selection Only

Sic Bo has no in-play decisions — just bet selection. The mathematically optimal play:

  1. Stick to Big/Small or Odd/Even. 2.78% house edge — the lowest available.
  2. Avoid Triple bets unless playing for entertainment. 13.89% house edge regardless of payout.
  3. Skip Two-of-a-Kind bets entirely. Worst standard bet at 18.5% house edge.
  4. Specific number bets are middle-ground. 7.87% — acceptable if you specifically want to bet a number.
  5. Don't combine many bets at once. Each independent bet adds expected loss. More bets = more variance, not better odds.

The simplest profitable approach: bet Big or Small every round, ignore everything else. Long-run RTP of 97.22% is competitive with most table games.

Sic Bo FAQ

What is Sic Bo?

Sic Bo is an ancient Chinese dice game played with three dice. Players bet on various outcomes of the dice roll — totals, specific numbers, combinations, doubles, triples. The dealer rolls the dice in a shaker, reveals the result, and pays winning bets. Round resolves in 20–30 seconds.

What is the best Sic Bo bet?

Big or Small bets (total 11–17 or 4–10 excluding triples) have the lowest house edge at 2.78%. Odd/Even bets have identical 2.78% edge. Specific-number bets have 7.87% edge. Triples pay 180:1 but have 13.89% edge — entertainment bets, not value bets.

Why do Big/Small lose on triples?

This is how the casino builds its edge into Big/Small bets. Any triple (1-1-1 through 6-6-6) causes Big/Small and Odd/Even bets to all lose, regardless of what the sum would be. Without this rule, these bets would be 50/50 — with it, the house edge is 2.78%.

What is Lightning Sic Bo?

Lightning Sic Bo is Evolution Gaming's enhanced Sic Bo variant launched 2022. Standard rules plus random multipliers (5x to 1000x) applied to specific dice outcomes each round. RTP is 96.23% on standard bets — slightly lower than standard Sic Bo due to the multiplier cost — but variance is much higher.

Can I count or predict in Sic Bo?

No. Each roll is independent. There's no card-counting equivalent for dice. Pattern-watching in past rolls doesn't predict future rolls. Don't pay for "Sic Bo prediction" apps — they don't work.

Should I bet on Specific Triples for the 180:1 payout?

Only for entertainment. The probability of a specific triple is ~0.46% per round. Even at 180:1 payout, the house edge is 13.89%. Long-run, you lose. Treat triple bets as small entertainment wagers, not as the core strategy.

Sic Bo vs Roulette — which is better?

European Roulette has 2.7% house edge across all standard bets. Sic Bo's Big/Small bets have 2.78% edge — very similar. Sic Bo has faster rounds (25s vs 40s) and more bet variety, but worse house edges on most non-main bets. Pick based on whether you prefer wheel-spin theatre or dice-roll variety.
