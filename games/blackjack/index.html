Rules of Blackjack

Blackjack is played between you and the dealer. The goal: get a hand value closer to 21 than the dealer's without exceeding 21 ("busting"). Both you and the dealer are dealt two cards initially; you can take additional cards to improve your hand, then the dealer plays. Best hand wins.

  1. You place your bet before any cards are dealt. Bet limits vary; standard tables run from ₹100 to ₹50,000 per hand.
  2. The dealer deals two cards face-up to you and two to themselves (one face-up, one face-down — the "hole card").
  3. If your first two cards total 21 (Ace + 10-value card) — that's a "blackjack" or "natural", which usually pays 3:2.
  4. If neither of you has blackjack, it's your turn to play. You choose to hit (take a card), stand (keep what you have), double down (double your bet and take one more card), split (if you have a pair, split into two hands), or surrender (give up half your bet, available at some tables).
  5. The dealer reveals their hole card and plays according to fixed rules (typically: must hit on 16 or lower; must stand on 17 or higher).
  6. Compare hands. Highest hand under 22 wins.

Aces are flexible — a hand of A+6 can be played as 7 or 17 depending on which is better. A "soft" hand is one where the Ace counts as 11 (e.g. "soft 17" = A+6); a "hard" hand is one where the Ace counts as 1 or there are no Aces.

Player Actions Explained

Hit

Take another card. Keep hitting until you stand or bust.

Stand

Keep your current hand. The dealer then plays.

Double Down

Double your bet and take exactly one more card. Best when you have 10 or 11 against a weak dealer card.

Split

If your two cards are the same rank, split into two separate hands. Place an equal bet on the second hand. Each hand plays independently.

Surrender

Give up half your bet rather than play the hand. Available at some tables. Best on hard 16 vs dealer 9, 10, or Ace.

Insurance

Side bet offered when dealer shows an Ace. Pays 2:1 if dealer has blackjack. House edge ~7% — basic strategy says always refuse insurance.

Dealer Rules — Fixed and Unchangeable

The dealer has no choices. They follow fixed rules:

  • Must hit on hard 16 or lower.
  • Must hit on soft 17 (A+6) at "H17" tables. Or must stand on soft 17 at "S17" tables (better for the player).
  • Must stand on hard 17 or higher.
  • If the dealer busts (goes over 21), all remaining players win.

S17 tables (dealer stands on soft 17) have lower house edge than H17 tables. This is one of the most important table-selection criteria. Most Evolution standard blackjack tables are S17.

Payouts — 3:2 vs 6:5 Matters Hugely

Always play 3:2 blackjack — never 6:5 Some tables pay only 6:5 on blackjack (₹100 bet → ₹120 win) instead of the standard 3:2 (₹100 bet → ₹150 win). This single rule change increases the house edge by ~1.4 percentage points — turning a 0.5% game into a 1.9% game. Avoid 6:5 tables entirely.

Basic Strategy — The Decision Chart

Basic strategy is the mathematically optimal play for every possible hand combination against every dealer up-card. It was computed in the 1950s by mathematicians at the US Army's Aberdeen Proving Ground (later published as "The Optimum Strategy in Blackjack") and refined countless times since.

Quick rules (memorise these first)

  • Always stand on hard 17 or higher. Hitting risks busting; standing forces the dealer to make a hand.
  • Always hit hard 11 or less. You cannot bust.
  • Double down on 11 against any dealer card (10 if rules allow).
  • Double down on 10 against dealer 2 through 9.
  • Always split Aces and 8s.
  • Never split 10s or 5s. 20 is too good to split; 10s should be doubled.
  • Stand on hard 12+ against dealer 4, 5, or 6 (dealer is most likely to bust on these).
  • Hit on soft 17 or lower. Soft hands can't bust on a single hit.
  • Stand on soft 19 or 20.
  • Never take insurance. The bet has a ~7% house edge regardless of your hand.

The full basic strategy chart

Full chart: hard hands (no Ace), soft hands (with Ace), pairs. Memorise via flashcard apps or print the chart. Live blackjack tables don't penalise you for consulting a chart — you can keep it open on a second monitor while playing.

The simplest possible improvement If you do nothing else, learn these two rules: always split Aces and 8s, and never take insurance. These two changes alone move the average player from ~97% RTP toward ~98.5% RTP.

Can You Count Cards Online?

Card counting works by tracking the ratio of high cards (10s, Aces) to low cards (2s–6s) remaining in the deck. When high cards predominate, the player has an edge; you increase bets and play differently. Card counting can shift the house edge in the player's favour by 0.5–1.5% if executed perfectly.

Online, card counting is effectively impossible for two reasons:

  • RNG blackjack: shuffles after every hand. There is no "deck composition" to count.
  • Live blackjack: uses 6–8 deck shoes with a high cut card position (the dealer reshuffles before getting deep enough into the shoe for counting to matter). Many tables now use continuous shuffling machines (CSMs) — re-inserting played cards back into the deck — eliminating counting entirely.

Don't pay for online counting "courses" or "edge sorting" claims. They don't beat modern online blackjack.

Blackjack FAQ

What is the house edge in blackjack?

With perfect basic strategy on a standard 6–8 deck game, house edge is approximately 0.5% (RTP 99.5%) — the lowest of any casino game. Without basic strategy, the house edge typically rises to 2–3%. Rule variations affect the exact number.

Can you count cards in online blackjack?

No, not effectively. RNG blackjack reshuffles after every hand. Live dealer blackjack uses 6–8 deck shoes with a high cut card position, and many tables now use continuous shuffling machines. Card counting requires offline conditions that online tables don't provide.

What is the best blackjack variant?

Classic Live Blackjack from Evolution or Pragmatic Live with standard rules (dealer stands on soft 17, blackjack pays 3:2, double after split allowed) gives the best RTP — 99.5% with basic strategy. Avoid variants that pay 6:5 on blackjack — they have significantly worse house edge.

Should I ever take insurance?

No. Insurance is a side bet with ~7% house edge. Basic strategy says always refuse insurance, even when you have a blackjack of your own (some tables offer "even money" which is mathematically the same bad bet).

What is the difference between Infinite and Classic Blackjack?

Infinite Blackjack (Evolution) allows unlimited players to play the same dealer hand simultaneously — no waiting for table seats. Each player makes their own hit/stand decisions on the same starting cards. RTP and rules are otherwise identical to Classic.

Is online blackjack rigged?

Live blackjack from licensed providers (Evolution, Pragmatic Live, Ezugi) uses physical cards on monitored tables. RNG blackjack uses certified Random Number Generators audited by independent agencies. No, it's not rigged at legitimate operators.
