What Is Dragon Tiger?

Dragon Tiger is a comparing card game played between two positions called Dragon and Tiger. Each side gets a single card from a standard 6 or 8 deck shoe; the higher-value card wins. Players bet on either Dragon, Tiger, or Tie. There are no third-card rules, no hand totals, no skill decisions — just one card per side.

Three things make Dragon Tiger popular at Indian-facing live casinos:

  • Speed. Rounds resolve in 20–30 seconds — significantly faster than blackjack, baccarat, or roulette.
  • Simplicity. No decisions to make once you bet. No rules to memorise.
  • Low house edge. 3.73% on Dragon or Tiger — better than American Roulette (5.26%) and comparable to many slots.

Dragon Tiger Rules — Step by Step

  1. The dealer shuffles a 6 or 8 deck shoe (depending on the table — Evolution typically uses 8, Ezugi uses 6).
  2. Betting window opens — typically 10–15 seconds. Place your bet on Dragon, Tiger, Tie, or any side bets.
  3. One card dealt to Dragon position — face up, in view of all players.
  4. One card dealt to Tiger position — face up.
  5. Higher card wins. No third-card rule. No hand combination. Just compare the two cards.
  6. Tie scenario — if both cards are the same rank (e.g. both 7s), Dragon and Tiger bets lose half their stake; Tie bets win 11:1.
  7. Bets are paid — winning Dragon or Tiger pays 1:1 (minus the half-loss on tie). Round ends.
  8. New round starts — typical interval is 25–30 seconds end-to-end.

Dragon Tiger FAQ

What is Dragon Tiger?

Dragon Tiger is a simplified one-card variant of baccarat that originated in Asian casinos. Two positions — Dragon and Tiger — each get one card. Whichever side draws the higher card wins. Round resolves in 20–30 seconds. House edge approximately 3.73% on Dragon or Tiger bet.

What is the Dragon Tiger RTP?

Dragon Tiger's RTP is 96.27% on Dragon or Tiger bets (3.73% house edge). The Tie bet has a much lower 67.23% RTP (32.77% house edge) — avoid it. Big/Small side bets are around 96.04% RTP.

Is there a Dragon Tiger strategy?

No. Dragon Tiger has zero in-play decisions. Dragon and Tiger are mathematically identical bets. The only strategic choice is: always bet Dragon or Tiger, never bet Tie, skip side bets. That's the entire strategy.

Should I ever bet on Tie?

No. Tie pays 11:1 but true odds are closer to 13:1, giving a 32.77% house edge — one of the worst bets in the entire casino. Even one of the live game shows with multipliers has better RTP than the Dragon Tiger Tie bet.

Dragon Tiger vs Baccarat — which is better?

For pure value, Baccarat's Banker bet (1.06% house edge) is significantly better than Dragon Tiger's 3.73%. Dragon Tiger compensates with faster rounds (25–30s vs 45–60s) and simpler mechanics. Pick based on whether you prioritise EV or pace.

Can I count cards in Dragon Tiger?

Theoretically yes — single-card-per-side games are simpler to count than baccarat — but the edge gained is tiny (~0.1% at best) and online live tables reshuffle frequently to defeat counting. Not worth attempting in practice.

Why is Ace low in Dragon Tiger?

Dragon Tiger uses straight rank values 1–13. Ace = 1 (lowest), King = 13 (highest). This is opposite of poker and blackjack where Ace is typically high. Card colour and suit don't matter — only rank.
