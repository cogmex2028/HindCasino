Affiliate Disclosure

This page explains, in plain language, how HindCasino earns revenue and how that revenue is structured to avoid compromising the editorial independence of our listings.

The Short Version

Our position HindCasino earns affiliate commission when a user registers at some listed operators via tracked links on our site. We disclose this on every page. Commission rates do not influence which operators we list, nor where they appear in our rankings.

How Affiliate Marketing Works on HindCasino

Many of the outbound links on HindCasino — particularly the "Visit [Casino]" and "Play Now" buttons — are affiliate links. When you click one and subsequently register an account at the destination casino, HindCasino may receive a commission from that operator. The commission is paid by the operator, not by you. Your costs as a player (deposits, bonuses, withdrawals) are identical whether you reach the operator via HindCasino or directly.

We label these links with the rel="sponsored nofollow" attribute, which is the standard Google recommends for disclosed commercial links. Some links on the site are not affiliate links — internal navigation, regulator references, and editorial citations are unpaid.

Commission Structures We Use

Online casino affiliate programmes typically pay using one or more of these structures:

  • Revenue share: a percentage of the operator's net revenue from referred players, paid monthly. This is the most common structure.
  • CPA (Cost Per Acquisition): a fixed one-time payment per qualifying new player.
  • Hybrid: a smaller CPA plus a smaller revenue share, combined.

We do not publish individual commission rates with each operator because those rates are commercially sensitive and vary by agreement. However, the editorial rules below ensure rates do not affect listings.

How We Maintain Editorial Independence

The following rules apply to every listing decision:

  1. Commission rate is not a factor in whether a casino is listed.
  2. Commission rate is not a factor in ranking position.
  3. We list operators that do not pay us commission if they meet our criteria. (At publication date, several smaller operators we have listed elsewhere fall into this category.)
  4. If an operator stops paying commission, we continue listing them as long as they meet our criteria.
  5. If an operator's licensing or audited-provider status changes (e.g. the licence is revoked, or providers are removed), we de-list them regardless of commission.
  6. We refuse "guaranteed top placement" commercial proposals.
  7. Authors and reviewers declare any direct financial interest in a listed operator. Where an interest exists, that author does not work on that operator's listing.

Full editorial policy is published on our Editorial Policy page.

What Affiliate Revenue Does Not Affect

The choice to list a casino is based on objective verification criteria:

  • Valid offshore licence with public regulator record
  • Audited game providers
  • Indian payment support (UPI etc.)
  • Bonus transparency and player-fair terms
  • Responsible-gambling tools

Operators that fail any of these checks are not listed, no matter how generous the commission offered. We have declined commercial agreements with operators that did not meet our criteria.

How to Identify Affiliate Links

You can tell which links are affiliate links in two ways:

  • The link uses rel="sponsored nofollow" — visible if you right-click → "Inspect element".
  • The link goes to a tracked URL containing affiliate ID parameters (typically with our partner network's domain in the redirect chain).

We also display an "Affiliate disclosure" badge in the hero section of every page that contains affiliate links, and link to this page from the footer of every page.

You Pay Nothing Extra

Affiliate commission is paid by the operator out of their marketing budget. As a player, your costs are identical whether you register via our site or directly with the operator. Bonus amounts, wagering requirements, and withdrawal terms are not changed by your point of arrival.
